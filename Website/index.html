The Problem with Traditional Utility Platforms

Traditional off-road utility vehicles are monolithic and often expensive to repair. Damage to a single section may require replacing entire components, increasing cost and downtime while generating unnecessary material waste.

The Ibex Solution

  • Modular Architecture – Replace only damaged sections
  • Terrain Adaptability – Forest, Rock, Sand, Snow (FRSS)
  • Snap-Fit Rear System – Customizable utility beds
  • Sustainable Lifecycle – Reduced material waste & downtime

Frequently Asked Questions

What problem does the IBEX FRSS solve?

Traditional utility vehicles are expensive to repair and difficult to adapt. The IBEX FRSS introduces a modular architecture that reduces waste, downtime, and replacement costs.

Why modular architecture?

Modular systems allow individual sections and utility components to be replaced or upgraded independently rather than replacing an entire platform.

Why electric?

Electric drivetrains reduce emissions, simplify maintenance, and support sustainable industrial deployment strategies.

What does FRSS stand for?

FRSS represents the four terrain environments the platform is optimized for: Forest, Rock, Sand, and Snow.
